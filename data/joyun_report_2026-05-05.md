## Executive Summary
The global and Indian skincare markets in early 2026 are heavily influenced by 'skinimalism' and a strong focus on skin barrier health, driven by science-backed ingredients like PDRN, exosomes, and postbiotics. Indian brands are actively expanding into body care and advanced sunscreens, while K-beauty continues its strong presence with a 'derm-luxury' approach, creating both competitive pressures and new opportunities for Joyun.

## Hot Ingredients

- **Exosomes** — Exosomes are rapidly gaining traction in K-beauty and clinical aesthetics, now moving into at-home topical products for advanced cellular repair, anti-aging, collagen/elastin stimulation, and inflammation reduction. _(Joyun: Joyun could explore exosome-mimicking botanicals or sustainable lab-grown versions to align with its vegan positioning and tap into the 'regenerative beauty' trend, offering cutting-edge cellular repair benefits.)_
- **Postbiotics** — Postbiotics, the non-living metabolic byproducts of fermentation, are redefining microbiome skincare. They offer stable and effective solutions for skin harmony and resilience, strengthening the skin barrier, boosting hydration, and reducing inflammation, especially for sensitive and compromised skin. _(Joyun: As a brand focused on vegan mucin and gentle formulations, postbiotics align well with Joyun's 'skin barrier' and 'skin health' focus, offering a stable and scientifically advanced ingredient to enhance existing products or create new ones.)_
- **Mushrooms (e.g., Tremella, Reishi, Chaga, Shiitake)** — Various mushroom extracts are gaining significant popularity as natural, multi-functional ingredients. They offer hydration (Tremella as 'vegan hyaluronic acid'), soothing, antioxidant, brightening, collagen support, and barrier repair benefits for diverse skin concerns. _(Joyun: Joyun, with its vegan focus, could leverage mushrooms, especially Tremella, as a powerful hydrating and soothing active, potentially as a 'vegan hyaluronic acid' alternative or complement to its ROSE-03 Vegan Mucin Essence.)_

Many Indian brands are launching affordable options, indicating a ripe market. → _Action: Develop a 'ROSE-XX Barrier Repair Cream' or 'Vegan Mucin Barrier Cream' incorporating ceramides, panthenol, and potentially postbiotics, positioned at an accessible price point (e.g., INR 400-600 / ~$4.80-7.20 USD)._
