Hot ingredients

Postbiotics / Microbiome-targeting ingredients

A major trend for 2026, shifting from probiotics to postbiotics to strengthen the skin barrier, reduce inflammation, and support long-term skin health without live cultures.

Joyun angle: Joyun can explore new product lines or re-position existing ones to highlight microbiome-balancing benefits, aligning with the 'skin barrier first' movement and gentle skincare.

Advanced Peptides

Peptide technology is evolving with more stable, targeted, and clinically effective formulations for collagen production, barrier repair, and anti-aging.

Joyun angle: Joyun already uses PDRN (a type of peptide); this trend offers an opportunity to expand into other targeted peptides or enhance the scientific messaging around ROSE-04's regenerative properties.

Exosomes

Emerging as a biotech innovation in professional skincare, focusing on cellular communication and regeneration, with a shift towards non-human, biotech-derived sources.

Joyun angle: While potentially a higher R&D investment, exosomes represent a future-forward ingredient for advanced anti-aging and regenerative formulations, aligning with Joyun's PDRN focus.

Algae-Derived Bioretinol

These retinoid-mimicking molecules from marine algae offer a gentle, plant-based alternative to traditional retinol, stimulating collagen and cell turnover without irritation.

Joyun angle: Aligns with Joyun's vegan ethos and can cater to consumers seeking effective anti-aging solutions suitable for sensitive skin, potentially expanding its 'gentle yet effective' positioning.

Opportunities

The 'skin barrier' and 'microbiome-first' approach is a dominant trend, with consumers seeking gentle, restorative cleansers and moisturizers.
