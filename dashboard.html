Hot ingredients

Exosomes

Rapidly emerging as a key ingredient for skin regeneration, elasticity, and wrinkle reduction, with significant industry investment and consumer search interest. Plant-derived exosomes are gaining traction.

Joyun angle: Joyun should monitor exosome technology closely, especially plant-derived versions, for future serum or essence formulations to enhance anti-aging and skin repair claims, potentially pairing with PDRN for synergistic effects.

PDRN

Continues to be a high-growth active, with K-beauty brands expanding its application into new formats like toner pads, eye patches, and cleansers, emphasizing skin vitality, moisture, and barrier health.

Joyun angle: As Joyun already uses PDRN, expanding into trending formats like PDRN toner pads or PDRN-infused cleansers could capture new market share and leverage existing ingredient expertise.

Centella Asiatica (Cica)

A staple in 'skin barrier' focused products, known for calming redness, supporting repair, and strengthening compromised skin. Widely used in serums, toners, and masks.

Joyun angle: Given Joyun's ROSE-03 essence, a Centella-focused essence or serum could complement its existing offerings, tapping into the strong 'skin barrier' trend and offering soothing benefits.

Peptides

Gaining traction for anti-aging, hydration, and improving skin elasticity. Increasingly found in moisturizers and specialized treatment pads.

Joyun angle: Joyun could explore peptide-rich formulations for new serums or moisturizers, aligning with the longevity and anti-aging positioning, potentially combining with existing actives.

Niacinamide

A versatile active for brightening, pore minimization, redness reduction, and barrier function improvement. Indian brands like Plum are launching Niacinamide-focused ranges.

Joyun angle: Joyun should consider Niacinamide as a hero ingredient for new product development, especially in cleansers or moisturizers, to address common Indian skin concerns like dullness and uneven tone.
